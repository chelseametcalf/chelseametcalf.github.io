About Me

Introduction

I'm Chelsea Metcalf, a first year Ph.D. student in computer science at the University of Florida. I'm doing research under Dr. Tuba Yavuz in the systems reliability lab. I like coding, photography, design, and traveling. My goal is to explore all 50 states and 196 countries. I currently have 14 states left to see.

Visited States: Alabama, Arizona, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Idaho, Illinois, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, West Virginia, Wisconsin, and Wyoming.

Research

I am doing work on software reliability. Last year (2014-2015), I worked on a project to automate the discovery of configuration parameters in Hadoop that might impact certain behavior. I used a static program analysis technique called Thin Slicing to extract relevant configuration parameters. Ultimately, the goal of the project was to provide suggestions on how to tune the relevant configuration parameters based on specific source-code level metrics. Another project I worked on this year (2015-2016) was to create a tool that detects changes in code that may introduce potential deadlocks. I used WALA as the static analysis framework for both of these projects.

Publications:
